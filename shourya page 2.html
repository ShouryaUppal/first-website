Water Ecosystems

Water ecosystems—such as rivers, wetlands, lakes, groundwater reservoirs, and coastal areas—play a vital role in maintaining clean, healthy water systems. Under SDG Goal 6: Clean Water and Sanitation, protecting and restoring these ecosystems is essential for ensuring long-term water security, supporting biodiversity, and enabling communities to thrive sustainably. Target 6.6: Protect and restore water-related ecosystems, including mountains, forests, wetlands, rivers, aquifers and lakes. Healthy water ecosystems: Naturally filter pollution and improve water quality, reduce flood and drought risks, Support plants, fish, and wildlife, Recharge groundwater sources, provide food, livelihoods, and cultural value, help regulate climate and store carbon. Human activities and climate change are placing increasing pressure on water ecosystems worldwide. Key Threats Include: Pollution from untreated wastewater and industrial waste, agricultural runoff (fertilizers, pesticides), deforestation and land degradation, over-extraction of water for farming and industry, urbanization and loss of natural habitats, climate change impacts—droughts, floods, rising temperatures

Some Strategies to protect and restore water ecosystems Aligned with SDG 6.6 are: Restoring rivers, wetlands and mangroves, reducing pollution through proper wastewater treatment, protecting natural buffers such as forests and riparian zones, implementing sustainable agriculture and reducing chemical runoff, monitoring water quality and ecosystem health, conserving groundwater and preventing over-extraction, using nature-based solutions (e.g., constructed wetlands). Social, environmental and economic benefits include cleaner water for communities, increased biodiversity and healthier habitats, natural flood protection, reduced cost of water treatment, eco-tourism and livelihood opportunities, climate resilience and reduced disaster risks. Some technologies and solutions supporting SDG 6.6 are real-time water quality monitoring sensors, watershed modeling and restoration technologies, remote sensing and satellite ecosystem mapping, nature-based wastewater treatment (e.g., reed bed systems), riverbank stabilization and erosion control systems, smart irrigation and water-saving technologies.
