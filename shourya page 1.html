Wastewater Treatment

Sustainable Development Goal 6 calls for universal access to clean water, safe sanitation, and responsible water management. Improving water quality through effective wastewater treatment and safe reuse is central to achieving this goal. By adopting modern technologies and sustainable practices, communities can reduce pollution, preserve water resources, and ensure safe, reliable water systems for everyone. Target 6.3 aims to “improve water quality by reducing pollution, eliminating dumping, minimizing hazardous chemicals, and substantially increasing recycling and safe reuse. Improving water quality is essential for: Reducing health risks and waterborne diseases, Protecting rivers, lakes, and coastal ecosystems, Ensuring sustainable supplies of clean water, Supporting agriculture, industry, and domestic needs, Meeting safe environmental discharge standards. Wastewater treatment is a key component of SDG 6 because untreated wastewater is a major source of pollution. Effective treatment systems remove contaminants, pathogens, and nutrients before releasing water back into the environment or reusing it safely.

Septic tanks and other domestic wastewater treatment systems are used by rural homeowners to treat wastewater from their homes. There are more than a million of these systems all over the world. The majority are septic tank systems, with some more complex filter systems and mechanical treatment plants. Domestic wastewater from toilets, sinks, showers, washing machines and dishwashers goes to the septic tank system. The solids settle out and form a sludge which needs to be emptied from time to time to prevent blockage. The liquid flows on through the tank and out into a network of shallow underground pipes (called a percolation area). The pipes have small holes that release the liquid so it can filter down through the soil. The liquid is naturally treated as it moves down through the soil before it reaches the groundwater below. The most basic type of system is a septic tank and percolation area. If a site is not suitable for a standard septic tank and percolation area, there are other options. You can add a filtering system to the septic tank, e.g. Soil and sand filters, Constructed wetlands, Willow bed evapotranspiration systems.
